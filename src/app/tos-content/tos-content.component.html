  1. Fixed yield rate

Users may connect their deposits with third party platforms (such as AAVE or DAI) to the Protocol in order to pool them with third party deposits and allow the balancing of the floating rate APY risk by generating through the Protocol a fixed yield rate.

Users may top up their deposits before maturation date instead of creating a new deposit, as described on the Platform. In addition, once a deposit is mature, Users may roll-over said deposit through the Platform to create a new deposit with a third party including the amount of the initial deposit plus the fixed-rate yield.

The Platform allows Users to customize the maturity date of the fixed-rate yield on their deposit by selecting a number of days after which they will be able to withdraw the amount of the deposit plus the fixed-rate yield. The amount of maturity offered is decided at the Foundations sole discretion. Alternatively, Users can mint zero coupon bonds (ZCBs) (i.e. deposits with preset maturities).

Deposits with third parties may be withdrawn without any Platform withdrawal fees once the maturity period is over plus the fixed-rate yield generated by the third party protocols. Users may also withdraw their deposits, entirely or partly, before the maturation date. Withdrawal before maturity date entails irrevocably forfeiting the fixed-rate yield as well as a Platform withdrawal fee of 0.5% of the deposited amount. A deposits maturity date refers to the last date of the deposits term as determined by the Users on the Platform. Holders of ZCBs may redeem their ZCB one-for-one with the underlying asset after maturity, however Users may not execute an early withdrawal for ZCBs.

Users understand that forfeited yield will continue to generate yield on third-party yield protocols and that the withdrawal fee will be distributed to Users who stake their MPH Tokens with third party staking providers, as per let. B ("Staking") below.

Deposits fixed-rate yields, rates and maturation options are determined at the Foundations sole discretion.

B.      Yield Tokens

Yield Tokens allow Users of the Protocol to profit from the rise in the yield rate or hedge part of their borrowing costs of a loan, e.g. Dai borrower on Compound would purchase cDAI YTs on the Platform. More specifically, Yield Tokens provide the token holders with a title of ownership on all future variable-rate yields generated by the corresponding deposits plus the purchase cost of the Yield Token.

 

Yield Tokens are minted by Users and only upon a purchase. In other words, Yield Tokens do not have any on chain representation or existence until a purchaser buys and mints them. In this framework, the Foundation will only offer the possibility to purchase and mint Yield Tokens.

 

Yield Tokens may only be purchased with the cryptocurrency used for the deposit, not with MPH Tokens. For example, if a user deposits DAI, then the Yield Tokens will need to be purchased with DAI.

 

As the Yield Tokens are tied to a deposit, the Yield Tokens pricing will vary depending on such deposit.

 

The yield payment to Yield Token holders is automatically triggered whenever part or all of the corresponding deposit is withdrawn by the third party depositor, and it is also possible to manually trigger it via the Claim Button in the User interface or by calling the contract functionDInterest.payInterestToFunders().

 

When the underlying deposit of a set of Yield Tokens is withdrawn before maturity, the Yield Token holders will receive a refund from the third-party platform, the amount of which is the minimum of an estimated lost yield calculated using the average floating yield rate and the fixed-rate yield offered on the withdrawn funds. If the deposit is withdrawn completely, the Yield Token holders will no longer receive interest payments and the Yield Token will be burnt, and a new Yield Token contract will be created when the User tops up the deposit in the future.

 

The Foundation will keep records of data historic in relation to third-party variable rate yield platforms and transactions made through the Protocol. This being said, the Foundation does not store any of these data. It is stored directly on chain.
